HyperTensor Research

Geometry-aware inference for large language models.

A small set of papers exploring what happens when you treat a trained transformer's weights as a low-dimensional manifold and design inference around that geometry rather than around the ambient parameter count.

By William Ken Ohara Stewart (NagusameCS) · 2026 · Source

Papers

Four papers under the HyperTensor project.

They go from concrete to speculative. Paper 1 is the empirical headline result. Paper 2 documents the production compression pipeline that the runtime actually ships with. Paper 3 looks at composition with speculative decoding and Attention Residuals. Paper 4 is the theoretical companion (simulation only, no LLM weights).

Background article An Introduction to the HyperTensor Papers (For Readers With No Background)

Long-form teaching article. Assumes no prior knowledge. Covers tensors, neural networks, transformers, attention, the KV cache, GPU bandwidth and cache hierarchy, quantisation and GGUF, PCA / SVD / Eckart‑Young, manifolds and intrinsic dimension, and ends with one plain-English summary per paper plus a vocabulary cheat-sheet. Read this first if any term in the four papers is unfamiliar.

Type: Article, not a research paper Length: ~30 minute read
Paper 1 Calibration-Free Low-Rank Attention Compression for Bandwidth-Bound LLM Decode

A weight-geometry-only PCA basis reduces attention $Q/K/V$ rank from $d{=}4096$ to $k{=}1024$ and produces a measured 106.27% of baseline decode throughput on a single consumer GPU, statistically significant at $p \approx 10^{-10}$. We argue (with caveats) that the speedup comes from the attention working set fitting in L2.

Model: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct Q4_K_M GPU: RTX 4070 Laptop
Paper 2 Geodesic Projection: A Production Compression Pipeline for LLM Inference

The full multi-slot Geodesic Projection (GP) compression scheme that the geodessical runtime ships with: per-layer PCA bases for $Q/K/V/O$, FFN up/gate, and a dedicated SVD path for FFN down. Includes the persistent geometry cache, the depth-sink shortcut, and the deployment envelope on a single 8 GB GPU.

Reference model: Llama-3.1-8B Q4_K_M Other models: design-validated only
Paper 3 Composing Compression: Geodesic Speculative Decoding and Attention Residuals

How a compressed-manifold model serves as a draft generator for speculative decoding against the full-precision transformer, and how Attention Residuals (AttnRes, Kimi Team 2026) compose with the compression. Design and runtime implementation; end-to-end benchmarks are not yet conducted.

Cites: Kimi AttnRes (arXiv:2603.15031)
Paper 4 Organic Training Theory and Geodesic Trajectory Caching

A theoretical framework that motivates Papers 1\u20133 and proposes future directions: a Riemannian-manifold view of the trained latent space, an $\mathcal{O}(nk^2)$ geodesic forward pass, and Geodesic Trajectory Caching (GTC) — a self-improving library of inference paths corrected by Jacobi fields. Validated only at simulation scale; the diffeomorphism construction is an explicitly open problem.

Open problem: Diffeomorphism $\phi$
Naming note. Earlier drafts used "GRC" for two different things. On this site, GRC = Geodesic Runtime Compression (the implemented low-rank attention scheme of Paper 1). The trajectory-library idea from the theory companion (Paper 4) is renamed GTC — Geodesic Trajectory Caching to remove the collision.

Scope
