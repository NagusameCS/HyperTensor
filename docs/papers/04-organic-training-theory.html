Paper 4 · Theory + Simulation

Organic Training Theory and Geodesic Trajectory Caching

By William Ken Ohara Stewart (NagusameCS) · HyperTensor Research, 2026

Read this first \u2014 framing of this paper

Papers 1, 2, and 3 are empirical. They report measurements on real hardware running real LLMs. This paper is different. It is a theoretical framework with a 25-test simulation suite and no LLM-scale validation. Where it claims a speedup figure (e.g. 4800\u00d7 at $n{=}32{,}768$), that figure is conditional on solving an open problem (the diffeomorphism $\phi$ that absorbs softmax and LayerNorm into Christoffel curvature). We flag the open problem at the front rather than the back so the reader is not surprised. This paper exists to motivate the empirical papers and to lay out a research direction; it should not be read as a benchmark-leaderboard claim.

\u00a70 \u2014 Abstract

One paragraph

We treat the trained latent space of a transformer as a Riemannian manifold $\mathcal{M}_\theta$ of intrinsic dimension $k \approx 30\u201350$, equipped with a Fisher-information metric. Under this view, inference is approximately the solution of the geodesic equation, with cost $\mathcal{O}(nk^2)$ rather than the standard $\mathcal{O}(n^2 d L)$. We further propose Geodesic Trajectory Caching (GTC): a self-improving library of stored geodesics, where new queries are served by applying a Jacobi-field linear correction to the nearest stored trajectory at cost $\mathcal{O}(k^2)$ per query. We connect this framework to Block Attention Residuals (AttnRes, Kimi Team 2026) by interpreting AttnRes weights as depth-wise geodesic segment selection on $\mathcal{M}_\theta$. All claims are validated only at simulation scale; the construction of the diffeomorphism $\phi$ that would make the framework deployable is left as an open problem.

\u00a70.5 \u2014 Naming

"GRC" disambiguation

The earlier draft of this paper used "GRC" for Geodesic Resonance Caching. Paper 1 on this site uses "GRC" for Geodesic Runtime Compression \u2014 a different, implemented thing. To remove the collision, the trajectory-library idea is renamed in this paper to Geodesic Trajectory Caching (GTC). The "resonance" terminology was always slightly metaphorical; the substantive content is the trajectory library plus the Jacobi correction, which the new name reflects more accurately.

\u00a71 \u2014 Organic Training Theory

The manifold view

Let $\theta \in \mathbb{R}^P$ be the trained weights. Define

$$\mathcal{M}_\theta = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^d : x = f_\theta(\text{tokens}) \text{ for some input}\}$$

with metric approximated by the Fisher Information matrix. Empirical intrinsic dimension estimation (PCA, TwoNN) on activation spaces gives $k \approx 30\u201350$ for current LLMs despite $d \in \{4096, 8192\}$. Inference under this view solves the geodesic equation with cost $\mathcal{O}(nk^2)$.

This section will reproduce the OTT material from the original PDF, with citations to the linear-representation-probing literature that supports the low-dimensional structure claim.

\u00a72 \u2014 Geodesic Trajectory Caching

Storing geodesics, correcting cheaply

Each completed inference produces a full geodesic trajectory, not just an answer. We propose storing these trajectories \u2014 the embedding, contextual velocity, waypoint sequence, Jacobi propagator $J(\lambda) \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$, an injectivity radius estimate, and the terminal logits \u2014 in a library indexed by nearest-neighbour search. New queries within the validity radius are served by a single $\mathcal{O}(k^2)$ matrix-vector product:

$$x^\mu(\lambda) = \bar{x}^\mu(\lambda) + J(\lambda) \cdot \delta q + \mathcal{O}(\|\delta q\|^2)$$

The Jacobi equation is linear, so a batch of similar queries can be corrected in a single matmul \u2014 a "resonance" effect where throughput rises rather than falls under load. The simulation suite verifies linearity (err $7.3 \times 10^{-13}$), superposition ($1.6 \times 10^{-17}$), and exactness in flat regions ($5.6 \times 10^{-17}$). On a synthetic mixture-of-Gaussians query distribution, the library converges to a 92% hit rate while storing only 7.8% of seen queries.

\u00a73 \u2014 Connection to Attention Residuals

AttnRes as depth-wise geodesic segment selection

Block AttnRes (Kimi Team, arXiv:2603.15031) replaces fixed PreNorm residual accumulation with a softmax over learned pseudo-queries against block summaries. Under the manifold interpretation, block summaries $b_n$ are waypoints on the geodesic, and the AttnRes attention weights $\alpha_{n \to l}$ select a convex combination of those waypoints. This re-anchors the hidden state to $\mathcal{M}_\theta$, mitigating the $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{L})$ magnitude inflation that PreNorm produces. Importantly, this connection is testable: AttnRes weights should concentrate on the block whose cached representation is geodesically nearest to the current state. We verify this in the simulation suite (8/8 trials).

The HyperTensor runtime ships an independent implementation of AttnRes (--attnres); Paper 3 reports its empirical interaction with compression. Theory and practice meet there.

\u00a74 \u2014 Open problems

What is missing

  1. The diffeomorphism $\phi$. An explicit map absorbing softmax and LayerNorm into Christoffel curvature is not constructed. Until $\phi$ is constructed, OTT is a framework, not a deployment.
  2. Geodesic initial velocity $v_0$. Computing the contextual direction without running full attention requires a closed-form expression for $v_0$ in terms of the Christoffel symbols and the input embedding. Approximations exist; rigorous derivation does not.
  3. Jacobi propagator construction cost. Building $J(\lambda)$ requires $\mathcal{O}(k \cdot N_w \cdot k)$ operations per record. At library-construction time this is non-trivial.
  4. AttnRes + GTC joint training. If GTC is to correct AttnRes block summaries via Jacobi fields, the training objective must encourage Jacobi-smooth trajectories in block-summary space.
  5. Injectivity radius estimation. Estimating $\rho(\bar{q})$ cheaply per record needs local curvature information that is expensive from scratch.
Status

Where this paper sits

Honest framing: theoretical proposal with simulation tests. Speedup numbers are conditional on the open problems above. Treat it as a research direction, not as a benchmark claim. The HyperTensor empirical papers (1, 2, 3) implement what is currently implementable from this framework: Paper 1 the calibration-free basis, Paper 2 the multi-slot manifold compression, Paper 3 the composition with AttnRes. GTC itself is unimplemented.
