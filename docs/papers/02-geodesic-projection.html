Geodesic Projection: A Production Compression Pipeline for LLM Inference

By William Ken Ohara Stewart (NagusameCS) · HyperTensor Research, 2026

Drafting

This paper is being assembled from work that already exists in the geodessical runtime. The numbers exist; the writeup does not, yet. What follows is the planned outline. Sections marked "data exists" have measurements in the repository already; sections marked "to be measured" need a benchmark pass before the paper is publishable.

\u00a70 \u2014 Abstract (planned)

What this paper will say

Paper 1 isolates a single design choice (calibration-free, attention-only, fixed rank) and measures throughput on one model. The HyperTensor runtime ships with a more general compression pipeline: Geodesic Projection (GP), a per-layer, per-matrix compression scheme covering all of Q, K, V, O, FFN up, FFN gate, and (via a dedicated SVD path) FFN down. The pipeline includes a persistent geometry cache, an OTT depth-sink shortcut, and an early-cache fast path that takes 70B-class startup from minutes to under a minute. We characterise GP across SmolLM2-135M, Phi-3.5-mini, Llama-3.1-8B, and a 70B target.

Outline

Sections, in order

1. From Paper 1 to GP

Paper 1 stops at attention-only and rank=$k$ shared across layers. GP generalises along three axes: per-matrix slots, per-layer rank selection (MCR/Ricci heuristic with a $k$-floor), and a separate FFN down SVD path. We lay out the seven slots and why the FFN-down path is special. Data exists.

2. The persistent geometry cache

The runtime serialises the discovered manifold geometry to ott_geometry.bin and the per-matrix projection bases to a W_proj cache, both keyed by a hash of the model weights and the configured rank. With both caches warm, Llama-3.1-8B starts in under a second; the 70B target moves from \u224870 minutes (full axiom run) to under a minute (cache hit). We document the cache layout, the geometry-peek shortcut for the depth-sink layer, and the safety check on cache invalidation. Data exists.

3. Per-model results

Cross-model walk: SmolLM2-135M (k=509, 88.37%, fluent text at 165 tok/s), Phi-3.5-mini-3.8B (rank=128, ~13.7 tok/s but quality degraded \u2014 honest negative result: 91.7% compression is too aggressive on $d{=}3072$), Llama-3.1-8B-Q4_K_M (the Paper 1 numbers, plus FFN-included variants), and the 70B target (startup, VRAM budget, speedup vs CPU-FFN-via-PCIe baseline). Data exists; needs cleanup.

4. Where GP wins and where it loses

A frank quality\u2013speed table. GP at low rank on small-$d$ models produces incoherent text; GP at moderate rank on large-$d$ models is a clean throughput win. The MCR/Ricci layer-rank heuristic needs a floor (the SmolLM2 k=509 fix) on small models. We describe the failure mode (attention collapse at $k/d < 0.4$ for $d \le 1024$) and the fix.

5. Origin: TensorOS

The axiom-discovery and manifold-extraction pipeline that GP relies on was first explored in the predecessor project, TensorOS. We document which pieces survived the move into HyperTensor (manifold extraction, geometry caching), which were rebuilt (the GPU upload path, the seven-slot scheme), and which were dropped. To be summarised once the relevant TensorOS files are pointed at.
