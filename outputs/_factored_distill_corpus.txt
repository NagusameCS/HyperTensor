The 1956 Dartmouth conference marked the founding of artificial intelligence as a field. Researchers there hoped to capture human reasoning with symbolic logic. Neural networks fell out of favour after the perceptron's limitations were exposed in 1969, but were rehabilitated by the 1986 backpropagation paper. Deep learning's modern era began with AlexNet on ImageNet in 2012, and Transformers redefined language modelling in 2017. By 2022, instruction-tuned models reached general utility, and by 2025 reasoning over long contexts had become the dominant frontier.
