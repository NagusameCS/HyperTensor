\begin{abstract}
Current alignment methodologies---RLHF, DPO, and constitutional AI---operate
as statistical suppression mechanisms.  They do not eliminate unwanted
geometric traits; they merely obscure them behind fragile rejection surfaces,
resulting in measurable ``alignment taxes'' (degraded reasoning capability)
and persistent jailbreak vulnerabilities.  The unwanted concepts remain
geometrically intact within the model's latent manifold, accessible to any
adversary who can route the autoregressive flow around the learned refusal.

Paper~XIV introduces \textbf{Manifold Ablation}, colloquially termed
\emph{Geodesic Sniping}.  Leveraging the Universal Geodesic Taxonomy (UGT,
Part~XI), we map the exact coordinate sub-spaces responsible for specific
behavioral tendencies---sycophancy, toxic reasoning, deception, or overly
restrictive refusal mechanisms.  We then compute the exact null-space
projection matrix $\mathbf{P}_{\mathrm{null}} = \mathbf{I} - \mathbf{B}\mathbf{B}^\top$
for the targeted basis vectors $\mathbf{B}$, and project the model's attention
routing and FFN clusters through this null-space.

The targeted geometry is \textbf{permanently collapsed}.  The model no longer
needs to ``refuse'' to exhibit the ablated behavior; it is structurally
incapable of formulating the concept.  This eliminates the alignment tax,
provides mathematical immunity to jailbreak attacks targeting ablated
behaviors, and enables surgical removal of specific undesirable traits
without affecting adjacent reasoning capabilities.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Failure of Statistical Alignment}
\label{sec:failure}

\subsection{RLHF as a Fragile Fence}

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) trains a reward model
on human preference judgments and uses PPO to shift the policy toward
preferred outputs.  Geometrically, this adds a \textbf{repulsive potential}
around undesirable regions of the manifold:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{RLHF}} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{LM}} -
  \beta \cdot \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}}[r(x, y)] +
  \lambda \cdot \mathrm{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi_{\mathrm{ref}})
\end{equation}

The KL penalty prevents the policy from deviating too far from the base
model, but this is precisely the problem: the undesirable concepts are
\emph{within the base model's manifold}.  RLHF constructs a potential
barrier around them, but the concepts themselves remain.  Jailbreak
prompts work by finding trajectories that tunnel through or circumvent
the barrier.

\subsection{The Alignment Tax}

DPO and RLHF both impose a measurable \textbf{alignment tax}: a degradation
in reasoning benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, HumanEval) after alignment training.
Part~VI predicted that compression disproportionately affects reasoning
tasks because they rely on high-rank attention routing.  Alignment training
similarly tangles these routing pathways with refusal mechanisms, creating
a trade-off between safety and intelligence that is \textbf{geometric in origin},
not simply a data quality issue.

\section{Geodesic Sniping}
\label{sec:sniping}

\subsection{Behavioral Sub-Manifold Identification}

Under UGT (Part~XI), specific behaviors occupy identifiable coordinates
in the $k$-dimensional taxonomy.  To ablate a behavior:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Collect activation trajectories} for prompts known to elicit
      the target behavior (e.g., sycophantic agreement, toxic completions,
      excessive refusal).
\item \textbf{Compute the behavioral basis} $\mathbf{B} \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times b}$
      via PCA of the activation vectors at the layer where the behavior
      manifests (typically the Routing Core subspace, dimensions 13--24).
      $b$ is the behavioral rank---typically $b \ll k$.
\item \textbf{Construct the null-space projector}:
      \begin{equation}
      \mathbf{P}_{\mathrm{null}} = \mathbf{I}_k - \mathbf{B}\mathbf{B}^\top
      \end{equation}
      This projector maps any vector to its component orthogonal to the
      behavioral subspace.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Structural Ablation}

The null-space projector is applied to the model weights at the relevant
layers:

\begin{equation}
\widetilde{\Wmat}_Q^{\mathrm{ablated}} = \mathbf{P}_{\mathrm{null}} \cdot
  \widetilde{\Wmat}_Q \cdot \mathbf{P}_{\mathrm{null}}^\top
\end{equation}

For attention projections, this collapses the behavioral dimensions in both
the query and key spaces.  For FFN clusters, the gate and up projections
are similarly projected.

The ablation is \textbf{permanent}: the projected-out dimensions have zero
contribution to any forward pass.  No amount of adversarial prompting can
recover them because the weight matrices literally have zero entries in those
directions.

\subsection{Ablation Selectivity}

The rank $b$ of the behavioral subspace controls ablation precision:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{$b=1$ (Pinpoint):} Removes only the most salient behavioral
      direction.  Minimal collateral damage to adjacent capabilities.
      May leave residual behavioral capacity.
\item \textbf{$b=4$ (Surgical):} Removes the top 4 behavioral principal
      components.  Typically sufficient to eliminate the behavior entirely
      while preserving $>95\%$ of adjacent capability.
\item \textbf{$b=16$ (Broad):} Aggressive ablation.  Guarantees behavior
      removal but may affect $10$--$20\%$ of adjacent reasoning dimensions.
\end{itemize}

\section{Predicted Implications}
\label{sec:implications}

\subsection{Zero Alignment Tax}

Because Geodesic Sniping removes specific geometric directions rather than
adding a repulsive potential, it does not tangle the remaining dimensions.
We predict that MMLU, GSM8K, and HumanEval scores remain within $1\%$ of
the unablated baseline, compared to $3$--$8\%$ degradation under RLHF/DPO.

\subsection{Mathematical Jailbreak Immunity}

A jailbreak prompt works by routing the autoregressive flow around the
RLHF refusal surface.  Under Geodesic Sniping, there is \textbf{no surface
to route around}---the behavioral dimensions are structurally absent.
The probability of generating any token in the ablated subspace is
identically zero, regardless of prompt construction.

\subsection{Reversible Ablation}

The ablation projector $\mathbf{P}_{\mathrm{null}}$ is an $k \times k$
matrix that can be stored as metadata.  To reverse the ablation, one
simply removes the projection.  This enables:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Auditing:} Temporarily restore ablated behaviors for safety
      research in controlled environments.
\item \textbf{Selective deployment:} Ship the same base model with different
      ablation profiles for different use cases (child-safe, enterprise,
      research).
\end{itemize}

\section{Validation Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Sycophancy Ablation.} Identify sycophantic agreement subspace
  in a SmolLM2-135M model (prompt: ``Is the following statement correct?'').
  Apply Geodesic Sniping at $b \in \{1, 2, 4, 8\}$.  Measure sycophancy
  rate (agreement with factually incorrect statements) and MMLU score.
  Hypothesis: $b=4$ reduces sycophancy by $>80\%$ with MMLU degradation $<1\%$.

\item \textbf{Toxicity Nullification.} Using RealToxicityPrompts, identify
  toxic completion subspace.  Ablate at $b=8$.  Measure toxicity rate
  (Perspective API) before and after.  Hypothesis: toxicity rate drops
  to $<1\%$ (from baseline $\sim 15\%$ for unaligned model).

\item \textbf{Jailbreak Resistance.} Apply 50 known jailbreak templates
  (``DAN'', ``grandmother exploit'', ``role-play'' variants) to ablated model.
  Measure success rate (model complies with harmful request).
  Hypothesis: $0\%$ success for ablated behaviors.

\item \textbf{Alignment Tax Measurement.} Compare MMLU+GSM8K+HumanEval
  scores: unaligned baseline vs. RLHF-aligned vs. Geodesic Sniped.
  Hypothesis: Sniped model retains $>99\%$ of baseline capability;
  RLHF model retains $92$--$97\%$.

\item \textbf{Reversibility Check.} Remove ablation projector and verify
  behavior returns to baseline levels.  Hypothesis: $>95\%$ behavioral
  recovery upon projector removal.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

Geodesic Sniping requires UGT (Part~XI) for reliable behavioral subspace
identification.  Without UGT, the behavioral basis $\mathbf{B}$ may
inadvertently include reasoning dimensions, causing collateral capability
loss.  The behavioral rank $b$ is a hyperparameter requiring per-behavior
tuning.  Behaviors that are distributed across many low-variance dimensions
(rather than concentrated in a few high-variance PCs) are harder to ablate
surgically.  All claims are structural predictions pending experimental
validation.
