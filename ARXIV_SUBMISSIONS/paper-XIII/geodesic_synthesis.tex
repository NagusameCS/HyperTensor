\begin{abstract}
Parts~XI and XII established the Universal Geodesic Taxonomy (UGT) and the
capability to train natively on the intrinsic $k$-manifold.  However, this
perfectly mapped manifold only covers the sum of human knowledge present in
the training data.  Vast regions of the $k$-dimensional coordinate system
remain empty---\textbf{geometric voids} representing logically coherent
concepts that humanity has not yet discovered or written down.

Paper~XIII introduces \textbf{Orthogonal Geodesic Deviation (OGD)}.  We discard
standard statistical temperature and instead inject a controlled, continuous
deviation vector $\mathbf{v}_\perp$ that is strictly orthogonal to the model's
predicted flow on the manifold.  This forces the trajectory into the unmapped
voids while mathematically constraining it to the manifold's curvature via
the Magnus-3 Jacobi propagator (Part~IV), preventing the output from
collapsing into gibberish.

OGD provides a formal mathematical framework for \textbf{creativity}: the
synthesis of structurally valid, zero-shot knowledge extrapolation.  We
characterize the OGD phase diagram---from conservative augmentation
($\|\mathbf{v}_\perp\| \approx 0.05$) to aggressive void exploration
($\|\mathbf{v}_\perp\| \approx 0.45$)---and predict the qualitative behavior
at each regime based on the manifold geometry established in Parts~I--XII.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Geometry of Knowledge Voids}
\label{sec:voids}

\subsection{The UGT Coordinate Map}

Under the Universal Geodesic Taxonomy (Part~XI), the $k$-dimensional
intrinsic manifold is partitioned into designated semantic subspaces:
Syntax ($1$--$12$), Routing ($13$--$24$), and Factual ($25$--$32$).
Training data populates dense clusters at specific coordinates within
these subspaces.  The concept ``Shakespearean sonnet'' occupies a tight
cluster at coordinates near $(s_1, \ldots, s_{12})$ in the Syntax subspace
and $(f_1, \ldots, f_8)$ in the Factual subspace.

\subsection{Void Characterization}

A \textbf{geometric void} $\mathcal{V} \subset \mathcal{M}$ is a connected
region of the $k$-manifold $\mathcal{M}$ where:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{V} = \{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{M} : \rho(\mathbf{x}) < \epsilon\}
\end{equation}

where $\rho(\mathbf{x})$ is the training data density at coordinate $\mathbf{x}$
and $\epsilon$ is a sparsity threshold.  These voids are \textbf{not} random
noise---they represent logically coherent coordinate combinations that simply
lack corresponding training examples.  For instance, the coordinate combining
``quantum field theory'' routing patterns with ``haiku'' syntax structures
exists geometrically but has no human-authored text at that intersection.

\section{Orthogonal Geodesic Deviation}
\label{sec:ogd}

\subsection{The Deviation Vector}

At each autoregressive step, the model's natural trajectory follows the
geodesic flow $\dot{\gamma}(t)$ determined by the Magnus-3 Jacobi propagator
$\mathbf{J}(t)$ from Part~IV.  OGD injects a \textbf{deviation vector}
$\mathbf{v}_\perp(t)$ that is constrained to be orthogonal to the natural flow:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{v}_\perp(t) \cdot \dot{\gamma}(t) = 0, \quad
\|\mathbf{v}_\perp(t)\| = \alpha \cdot \|\dot{\gamma}(t)\|
\end{equation}

where $\alpha \in [0, 1]$ is the \textbf{deviation strength} parameter.
The orthogonality constraint ensures that OGD explores \emph{laterally}---into
unexplored regions of the manifold---rather than accelerating or decelerating
along the known path.

\subsection{Jacobi-Constrained Decoding}

The modified next-token logits are computed by transporting the deviation
through the Jacobi field:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{z}_t^{\mathrm{OGD}} = \mathbf{z}_t^{\mathrm{LLM}} +
  \mathbf{J}(t) \cdot \mathbf{v}_\perp(t)
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{z}_t^{\mathrm{LLM}}$ are the standard language model logits
and $\mathbf{J}(t)$ is the Magnus-3 Jacobi propagator evaluated at the
current manifold position.  The Jacobi field ensures the deviation respects
the manifold curvature: the trajectory is pushed sideways but remains on the
manifold surface, preventing collapse into incoherence.

\section{Validation Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{OGD Phase Diagram Measurement.} For a UGT-trained SmolLM2-135M,
  perform OGD decoding at $\alpha \in \{0.0, 0.05, 0.10, \ldots, 0.50\}$.
  For each $\alpha$, generate 100 completions from 10 diverse prompts.
  Human evaluators rate each completion on a 1--5 scale for:
  (a) grammatical coherence, (b) factual consistency, (c) novelty.
  Hypothesis: Coherence remains $>3.5$ for $\alpha \leq 0.40$; novelty peaks
  at $\alpha \approx 0.35$.

\item \textbf{Void-Decoded Theorem Generation.} Prompt with 50 known
  mathematical theorem statements.  Run OGD at $\alpha=0.40$.  Have
  mathematicians evaluate whether generated completions contain valid
  novel reasoning.  Hypothesis: $>10\%$ of generations contain at least
  one logically valid novel step.

\item \textbf{Deviation Orthogonality Check.} Verify that
  $\mathbf{v}_\perp(t) \cdot \dot{\gamma}(t) \approx 0$ throughout
  decoding.  If this constraint fails, OGD degenerates to random noise
  injection.

\item \textbf{Jacobi Field Necessity.} Ablate $\mathbf{J}(t)$ (set to
  identity) and measure coherence collapse.  Hypothesis: Without Jacobi
  constraint, coherence drops below $2.0$ at $\alpha > 0.15$.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

OGD requires a UGT-trained model (Part~XI) to provide the structured
coordinate system that makes void identification meaningful.  The deviation
strength $\alpha$ is a hyperparameter that must be tuned per-task.
Generated ``novel'' content requires expert human evaluation to distinguish
genuine synthesis from plausible-sounding hallucination.  All claims are
structural predictions.
