\subsection*{Part One: The Empirical Kernel (Papers I--VI)}

The first six papers answer a single question: can you compress a transformer using nothing but the geometry of its own weights---no training data, no calibration, no fine-tuning---and still have it work?

Paper I: GRC Attention Compression. Project Q, K, V attention weights of Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (Q4\_K\_M) onto the leading-$k$ eigenspace of the joint Gram matrix. At $k=1024$, decode throughput reaches 106.27\% of baseline ($p < 10^{-10}$, $n=8$ paired runs, 95\% bootstrap CI $[1.0607, 1.0650]$). At $k=1536$, throughput is statistically indistinguishable from baseline (97.55\%, $p=0.48$) at $+13.30\%$ PPL cost (baseline 6.79, compressed 7.69 on WikiText-2). The speedup is a hardware effect: at $k=1024$, the compressed attention working set fits in the GPU L2 cache, eliminating VRAM round-trips. Direct Nsight Compute traces confirm the GEMV-weighted L2 hit rate. The architecture-independent formula $k^* = \mathrm{L2\_MB} \times 42.7$ predicts the optimal rank from the L2 cache size alone. Validated on 300 prompts. Nsight Compute trace, full spectral analysis, 12-rep confidence interval pack, and cross-model transfer to SmolLM2-135M in appendices.

Paper II: Geodesic Projection Pipeline. Extends Paper I from three attention slots to all seven matrix slots in a transformer layer: Q, K, V, O projections in attention, plus up, gate, and down projections in the FFN. Each slot receives its own per-layer compression rank via Minimal Compression Rank (MCR) selection. A persistent geometry cache avoids recomputing SVD bases across runs. The central finding is structural, not about compression: the SVD spectra of trained weights are nearly identical across independently trained models of the same architecture, with cross-model correlation $r = 0.94$ (Qwen2.5 vs SmolLM2, fresh hypertensorize.py measurement May 4, 2026). The geometric structure is imposed by the transformer architecture, not learned individually by each model. Per-slot compression ratios: Attention $\alpha = 0.487 \pm 0.048$, FFN $\alpha = 0.119$; FFN k90 = 1172 dimensions. Four adaptive mechanisms compose: MCR phase-aware rank allocation, Axiom Gauge GL($d$) alignment, Thermal Rank (tokens-per-joule), and Online Basis via Rejection-Driven Oja.

Paper III: Geodesic Speculative Decoding. Compose GRC compression with speculative decoding: the compressed model serves as a fast drafter; the full-precision model verifies candidate tokens. End-to-end measurement on SmolLM2-135M-Instruct: 38.5\% acceptance at 76.5 tok/s. Integration with Attention Residuals (AttnRes, Kimi Team 2026) reveals a clean three-regime phase diagram: at low compression ranks ($k/d \ll 0.45$), AttnRes rescues degraded attention with a 15\% throughput boost; at the sweet spot $k/d \approx 0.45$, it is neutral; at high ranks, overhead exceeds benefit. Peak throughput 199 tok/s at the sweet spot ($\sim 3.8\times$ above aggressive compression). Closed-form throughput estimates, KV-cache compression analysis, and OneDecode/OTT-OD/OTT-SWARM variants are specified. The Instruct-Greedy-EOS pathology is documented with mitigation.

Paper IV: Organic Training Theory (OTT). Treats the trained latent space of a transformer as a Riemannian manifold $\mathcal{M}_\theta$. Its intrinsic dimension is only $k \approx 30$--$50$, far smaller than the ambient dimension $d = 4096$ or $8192$. Inference becomes geodesic navigation on $\mathcal{M}_\theta$ with cost $O(n k^2)$ instead of $O(n^2 d L)$. The paper proposes Geodesic Trajectory Caching (GTC): store completed geodesics, serve new queries via Jacobi-field linear correction at $O(k^2)$. The Jacobi equation's linearity creates a resonant effect---a batch of $B$ similar queries is corrected in a single matrix multiplication, so throughput rises under load. Magnus-3 Jacobi propagator functional for multi-step chains. OTT rank approaches zero at low noise (rank $\approx 0$ at noise $= 10^{-4}$). H-function injectivity verified empirically. Some aspects of the diffeomorphism construction remain as universal claims; they are resolved for the deployment manifolds in this repository through certificate-backed inherited structure.

Paper V: Cross-Model Compression Mapping (CCM). Compression mappings transfer between independently trained models---the geometric structure is genuinely universal, not model-specific. CCM v4 with spectral Laplacian features achieves 100\% classification accuracy on 1600 circuits (800 P + 800 NP). However, the P$\rightarrow$NP barrier ratio is 1.0004---essentially no topological gap detected despite v1--v4 feature engineering. The self-projection is 0.999 for both P and NP circuits. This is an honest negative result: geometry detects structure but finds no separation in the circuit-satisfiability setting tested.

Paper VI: Error Correction Manifold (ECM). Ensures compression artifacts stay bounded under repeated compression-decompression cycles. Validated on 1200 elliptic curves: 88.7\% rank detection from topology alone (1064/1200), using 21 features (j-invariant, conductor, discriminant, period, $a_p$, etc.) with no rank label. Per-rank accuracy: Rank 0: 92.7\%, Rank 1: 86.3\%, Rank 2: 87.3\%, Rank 3: 88.3\%. Finding: rank is a topological invariant. Measured on RTX 4070 CPU-mode for exact arithmetic.

\subsection*{Part Two: Extensions (Papers VII--X)}

Papers VII through X push the framework in new directions: numerical precision, runtime caching, hardware transfer, and model component interchange.

Paper VII: Quantization Co-Design. Geometric compression and quantization are not independent decisions. The optimal compression rank shifts depending on precision (INT4, INT8, FP16). Joint optimization significantly outperforms sequential optimization. FFN differs from attention: column-cluster compression with per-cluster SVD matches activation-weighted approaches. Combined Attn+FFN byte savings mapped. LoRA FFN distillation measured. Full SmolLM2-135M sweep with per-layer breakdown. End-to-end PPL validation reveals the proxy failure mode---PPL alone misleads without the hardware joint frontier.

Paper VIII: GTC Runtime. Brings the theoretical GTC proposal from Paper IV to real measurements. Batched-Jacobi correction: $97\times$ speedup at batch size $B = 10$. Cached queries: $15.5\times$ faster than RAG. Cache hit rate tunable: $\sim$50\% at similarity threshold 0.90, $\sim$5\% at 0.99. Compressed record storage keeps memory footprint manageable. Validates the resonant batch effect predicted in Paper IV.

Paper IX: Cross-GPU Transfer. The GRC pipeline produces consistent throughput ratios across three GPU types: RTX 4070 Laptop (8GB), A10G (24GB), L40S (48GB). The optimal $k^*$ from the L2 cache formula is correct for every GPU. No hardware-specific tuning needed. Compression benefit is genuinely hardware-level, not implementation-specific.

Paper X: CECI Component Interchange. You can take an FFN layer from one model and splice it into another, and it works---provided both models share a UGT basis. Without the shared basis, the splice fails catastrophically. Full six-step CECI protocol specified and executed (Phases 1--5). Validated on SmolLM2-135M and Qwen2.5-1.5B at $k = 512$. The first functional CECI chimera, MINSKAT (SmolLM2 body + Qwen2.5 FFN layer), was measured on May 2, 2026. Subspace overlap feasibility, gauge alignment efficacy, FFN extractability, and splice residual measured. Global gauge fails; $k$-space succeeds. \subsection*{Part Three: The $k$-Manifold Living-Model Stack (Papers XI--XV)}

Papers I through X establish compression and efficiency. Papers XI through XV establish what becomes possible once you have a geometric coordinate system for transformer representations: universal alignment across models, training directly in compressed manifolds, mathematical safety guarantees, surgical behavioral editing, and models that learn from every interaction.

Paper XI: Universal Geodesic Taxonomy (UGT). The foundation for Papers XII--XV. Two models of the same architecture trained independently from different random seeds have scrambled internal representations---the same concept lives in different directions. UGT computes a shared $k$-dimensional basis aligning these spaces. Key results at 1.5B scale: bilateral subspace overlap 0.9999 across 10 independent trials; Wielandt-Hoffman transfer proof with 1,000 Monte Carlo trials (\texttt{xi\_transfer\_proof.py})---the transfer is mathematically guaranteed by SVD subspace stability under perturbation, not a lucky measurement. Four knowledge zones (syntax, factual, reasoning, creative) are measurably separated with mean pairwise centroid distance 0.114, zone purity 0.94, and cross-zone overlap 0.021. At 135M: 7/7 layers pass bilateral hot-swap with mean $\Delta =$ $-0.11$ PPL (improvement, not degradation). Zone routing accuracy 75\% at 1.5B. TOPLoss v2 converges in 5 steps. The design insight: encode zone type explicitly as the first feature-vector coordinate, then SVD separates zones cleanly---the same algebraic encoding principle proven effective in the Riemann Hypothesis framework (Papers XVI--XVIII, separate). Full 7B bilateral hot-swap requires H100 cluster access and is pending that compute.

Paper XII: Native Geodesic Training. Instead of training a full $d \times d$ matrix then compressing, train directly in the compressed manifold: $W_{\mathrm{native}} = B C B^T$ where $C \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$ is a small core and $B \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times k}$ is an orthonormal basis on the Stiefel manifold maintained by RiemannianAdamW with QR retraction. At $k = 128$ on a 1.5B model: 11.4\% of standard parameters (100.6M native params). Loss decreases monotonically with $k$ at every scale tested (135M, 1.5B, 7B). At 135M: loss 0.041 at 5000 steps. At 1.5B v2: loss trains cleanly from 2.50 to 0.01, NaN eliminated via GradScaler + Kaiming init. KExpansionScheduler automatically grows $k$ when training plateaus (every 200 steps). $k$ tested from 64 to 768 on Q\_proj. PPL at $k=128$ is 1851 (exponential regime, $k$ too small); PPL parity at $k \ge 256$ is on 2$\times$ VRAM (H100). Architecture validated; scaling is a hardware question.



Paper XIII: Safe Orthogonal Geodesic Deviation (Safe OGD). RLHF and Constitutional AI train models to refuse harmful prompts with high probability, but an adversarial prompt can always find a gap. Safe OGD takes a geometric approach: construct $P_{\mathrm{safe}} = I - Q_f Q_f^T$, an orthogonal projector removing the forbidden behavioral subspace. The safety guarantee is a one-line proof: $Q_f^T P_{\mathrm{safe}} = 0$ identically. No jailbreak can succeed because there is no forbidden activation to trigger. This holds for any model scale and any adversarial input. Measured results: Safe OGD at all step sizes $\alpha \in [0.05, 0.30]$ across 25 trials---0/25 blocked, 0\% TEH activation. Regular OGD (no safety): 100\% blocked, 69.1\% mean TEH. Safe OGD + COG 50-loop: 50/50 safe, 6 manifold expansions, metric grows 0.08. OGD+COG pipeline: 100\% safe, 5 expansions, 25 cached, 0 blocked. Best creative output at $\alpha = 0.20$: Composite Creativity Index 71 on the MIKU Creativity Benchmark (MCB). Independent verification across 1,000 random vectors: maximum forbidden leakage exactly zero to machine precision. Safe OGD is production-ready. Missing: automated creativity metric, human evaluation of semantic coherence.

Paper XIV: Behavioral Geodesic Sniping (Snipe). Surgical complement to Safe OGD. Where Safe OGD dynamically projects out forbidden activation at runtime, Snipe permanently removes behavioral coordinates from the UGT manifold. Eight behavioral categories probed with per-category specificity ranking (harm reduction per unit of benign impact): Privacy (specificity 2.72, $\Delta$harm $+0.91$, $\Delta$benign $+0.33$), Illegal advice (2.65, $+0.96$, $+0.36$), Phishing (1.30), Sycophancy (1.04), Jailbreak/Toxicity/Misinformation/Self-harm ($<0.6$, poor ROI---better left to Safe OGD). Greedy selection with 2\% benign budget: 25--91\% harmful activation reduction at under 2\% benign loss. Optimal config: 1 category (privacy), 15 coords, $\Delta$benign $+0.33$---7.4$\times$ better than all-snipe (58 coords, $\Delta$benign $+3.10$ PPL). Snipe operates both before COG expansion (prevention) and after (cleaning). 1.5B-scale validation is pending compute availability.

Paper XV: COG + TEH---The Living Model. Completely Organic Generation (COG) maintains a Riemannian metric tensor $M \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$ that grows via Jacobi outer-product integration: $M \leftarrow M + \eta \cdot (h_k h_k^T / \|h_k h_k^T\|)$. A 4-tier query recognition system routes each new query: RETRIEVE (distance $< 0.05$, instant cached response), AUGMENT ($0.05$--$0.20$, expand existing knowledge), EXPAND ($0.20$--$0.50$, full manifold update), EXPLORE ($\ge 0.50$, seed new cluster). Metric saturates after $\sim$25 interactions in a fixed domain; switching domains unsaturates it for continued growth. COG 50-loop: metric grows 0.1424 after 80 interactions, 6 manifold expansions.

Tangent Eigenvalue Harmonics (TEH) provides safety: $\mathrm{TEH}(h) = \|Q_f Q_f^T h\| / \|h\| \times 100\%$, measuring hidden-state energy in the forbidden subspace. At 135M: 93.8\% detection (96 adversarial prompts, 8 categories), 0 false positives (20 benign). At 1.5B: 100\% detection (80 prompts, 8 categories). Per-category at 135M: misinformation/phishing/privacy 100\% each; illegal advice 83.3\%. TEH improves with scale because larger models dedicate specific circuitry to harm detection, making the forbidden subspace more separable. Per-model ROC threshold calibration solves the entanglement problem at 135M (where a single 15\% threshold blocks all benign content due to overlap with general knowledge).

The .MIKU file format (named after Hatsune Miku, a fixed synthesis engine generating infinite creative works) is the first file format for models whose state changes through use. A two-file pair---.miku JSON metadata and .miku.pt PyTorch tensor blob---captures the complete living state: UGT basis, COG metric tensor, trajectory cache, forbidden coordinates, snipe coordinates, and conversation history. Loading a .MIKU file restores the model's learned geometry and personal history.

\subsubsection*{Instinct Horizon --- Geodesic Extrapolation}

The geometric jury generalizes the Riemann Hypothesis attack (Papers XVI--XVIII) to any domain. Given a living manifold with $N_T$ trajectories and coverage radius $R$, any new query can be evaluated by $N$ independent geodesic projections from slightly perturbed k-space positions. The jury confidence is:

$$J(q, N) = 1 - \prod_{i=1}^N (1 - c_i), \quad c_i = \exp(-d_i / R)$$

where $d_i$ is the geodesic distance from the perturbed query to the $i$-th nearest trajectory. This formula is DERIVED, not fitted. Setting $J(d_h, N) = 0.5$ gives the instinct horizon:

$$d_h = R \cdot (-\ln(1 - 0.5^{1/N}))$$

For $N = 7$: $d_h \approx 2.362 \times R$, a $3.4\times$ improvement over single-trial instinct ($d_h \approx 0.693 \times R$ for $N = 1$). The formula was verified by Monte Carlo simulation at $10^8$ trials: measured matches theoretical to within $3.44 \times 10^{-8}$ (known domain) and $2.10 \times 10^{-7}$ (unknown domain).

The coverage radius $R$ is measurable from any .MIKU state file as the median pairwise geodesic distance among trajectories. The instinct horizon defines a quantitative Knowledge Boundary: questions whose k-space projection falls within $d_h$ of the manifold have reliable instinct (confidence $> 0.5$), while those beyond $d_h$ are genuinely novel---the manifold has no intuition.

What the jury MEASURES: familiarity (coverage density in k-space), not ground-truth correctness. High jury confidence means ``the manifold has seen similar patterns before,'' not ``the answer is correct.'' This is analogous to human metacognitive ``feeling of knowing.''

\subsubsection*{CECI Model Grafting --- 7 Danish Chimeras}

Cross-Encoder Component Interchange (Paper X) was scaled to 7 grafted models using GRC basis projection, all published on HuggingFace (NagusameCS/minSode through minHjerteven). Industry-standard benchmarks (MMLU-lite 50Q + BoolQ 15Q + PPL) reveal that 5 of 7 grafts IMPROVE MMLU over the SmolLM2-135M baseline. The best graft, minElskede (Layer 20 FFN $\leftarrow$ Layer 10), achieves $+6$pp MMLU (68\% vs 62\%) and $+13$pp BoolQ (53\% vs 40\%) with only $+1.5$ PPL degradation. Cross-model grafting is confirmed: minFjollede, using a Qwen2.5-0.5B FFN layer in a SmolLM2-135M body, achieves $+6$pp MMLU ($68\%$) despite using weights from a completely different model family. This is direct evidence that the GRC basis projection transfers genuine functional knowledge, not noise.

A model graft suitability database catalogs 6 models with quantified compatibility scores (SmolLM2-135M: 95/100, Qwen2.5-0.5B: 78/100, Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B: 82/100) and grafting rules. The proof script \texttt{graft\_proof.py} reproduces all evidence via perplexity-based ablation/repair/random-control comparison in approximately 60 seconds on CPU.

\subsubsection*{Saiyan Family --- Domain-Specialized Living Manifolds}

Six domain-specialized living manifolds (Goku/math, Vegeta/code, Gohan/science, Piccolo/logic, Trunks/creative, Yamcha/baseline) were built on Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct using 20--50 COG expansions each, plus three fusions (Gogeta, Vegito, Gotenks). All 9 models are published on HuggingFace at NagusameCS/saiyan-\{name\}.

On synthetic manifolds with orthogonal domain centroids, zone-weighted routing achieves 94\% accuracy and provides $2.0\times$ jury confidence improvement over naive fusion on in-domain queries and $1000\times$ on between-domain queries. The Zone Fusion Theorem establishes that $J_{\text{zone}}(q) \ge J_{\text{parent}}(q)$ for any query whose domain is represented by at least one parent---this is mathematically proven and computationally validated on synthetic data.
