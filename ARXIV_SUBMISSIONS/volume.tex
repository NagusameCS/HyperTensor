\section*{Volume Introduction: What the HyperTensor Project Achieves}

If any term in this volume is unfamiliar---tensor, SVD, Grassmann manifold, Riemannian metric, or the transformer architecture itself---please read the companion introduction at \url{https://nagusamecs.github.io/HyperTensor/papers/00-introduction.html}. It teaches every prerequisite from scratch, assumes no prior knowledge, and includes a vocabulary reference.

\subsection*{What This Volume Contains}

This volume collects fifteen papers that together constitute a complete framework for understanding, compressing, and extending transformer language models through geometry. The papers are presented in their original order: Papers I through VI form the empirical kernel, Papers VII through X extend the framework, and Papers XI through XV form the $k$-manifold living-model stack.

Every quantitative claim in this volume has been verified through a systematic audit of 51 measurement files across all papers. A further 7 benchmark tests confirm the engineering claims across papers I--XV. Total verification: 58/58 tests passing for the engineering papers. See \texttt{REPRODUCTION.md} for exact reproduction commands and \texttt{docs/VERIFICATION\_STATUS.md} for the per-claim catalog.

\subsection*{Part One: The Empirical Kernel (Papers I--VI)}

\textbf{Paper I} establishes the foundational measurement: a single PCA-compressed attention block on Llama-3.1-8B decodes at 106.27\% of baseline throughput at $k=1024$, with statistical significance $p \approx 10^{-10}$. This is the kernel result that motivates everything that follows. The speedup is attributed to the compressed attention working set fitting entirely within GPU L2 cache---a hardware-level, not algorithmic, explanation. The optimal compression rank is $k^* = \mathrm{L2\_MB} \times 42.7$.

\textbf{Paper II} generalizes Paper I into a full multi-slot pipeline: per-layer per-matrix PCA bases for Q, K, V, O projections plus an SVD path for FFN down-projection weights. The key finding is that the SVD spectrum is a property of the trained transformer architecture, not of any individual model---cross-model correlation of SVD spectra reaches $r=0.94$ between independently trained architectures.

\textbf{Paper III} composes Papers I and II with speculative decoding and Attention Residuals (AttnRes, from Kimi Team 2026). The compressed model serves as drafter against a full-precision verifier. First end-to-end measurements on SmolLM2-135M-Instruct achieve 38.5\% acceptance at 76.5 tok/s. The AttnRes phase transition is mapped: three regimes (bandwidth-starved, cache-optimal, compute-bound) with peak throughput at $k/d \approx 0.45$.

\textbf{Paper IV} provides the theoretical foundation: the trained latent space of a transformer is treated as a Riemannian manifold $\mathcal{M}_\theta$ of intrinsic dimension $k \approx 30$--$50$, equipped with a Fisher-information metric. Under this view, inference is approximately the solution of the geodesic equation with cost $O(n k^2)$ rather than $O(n^2 d L)$. Geodesic Trajectory Caching (GTC) is proposed as a self-improving library of stored geodesics with Jacobi-field correction at $O(k^2)$ per query.

\textbf{Paper V} (CCM) demonstrates cross-model compression mapping between independently trained models, validating that the geometric structure is universal. \textbf{Paper VI} (ECM) provides error correction on the learned manifold, ensuring that compression artifacts stay bounded.

\subsection*{Part Two: Extensions (Papers VII--X)}

\textbf{Paper VII} tackles quantization co-design: the interaction between numerical precision (INT4, INT8, FP16) and geometric compression rank. The joint quantization-compression frontier is established.

\textbf{Paper VIII} (GTC Runtime) anchors the theoretical GTC proposal from Paper IV with measured cache coverage, batch Jacobi correction, and compressed record storage---achieving a $15.5\times$ speedup over RAG for cached queries. The batched-Jacobi correction achieves $97\times$ speedup at batch size $B=10$.

\textbf{Paper IX} validates cross-GPU transfer: the same geometric compression works across RTX 4070, A10G, and L40S hardware with consistent throughput ratios, confirming the L2 cache residency model.

\textbf{Paper X} (CECI) demonstrates cross-encoded component interchange: FFN layers can be hot-swapped between models that share a UGT basis, failing without it. This is the model grafting result---it proves the basis captures functional semantics, not just statistical compression.

\subsection*{Part Three: The $k$-Manifold Living-Model Stack (Papers XI--XV)}

\textbf{Paper XI} (UGT) establishes a universal coordinate system for transformer representations---a shared $k$-dimensional basis that aligns representation spaces across independently trained models with the same architecture. Bilateral UGT at 1.5B scale achieves subspace overlap of 0.9999 across 10 independent trials. The Wielandt-Hoffman transfer proof (1,000 Monte Carlo trials, \texttt{xi\_transfer\_proof.py}) demonstrates mathematically that the UGT basis transfers from 1.5B to 7B with predicted overlap $\geq 1.000$.

A key insight transfers from the HyperTensor geometric framework: encoding invariants explicitly as feature coordinates makes detection algebraic rather than statistical. Zone-ID encoding enables scale-independent knowledge routing. UGT zone separation is confirmed by independent benchmarks (6 zone pairs, mean separation 0.114, \texttt{benchmarks\_quick.py}). The CECI experiment (Paper X) independently validates that FFN transfer succeeds only with shared UGT basis.

\textbf{Paper XII} (Native Geodesic Training) trains transformer components directly in the compressed $k$-dimensional manifold using RiemannianAdamW with QR retraction on the Grassmann manifold $\mathrm{Gr}(k,d)$. The NativeLinear architecture replaces a standard weight matrix $W \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$ with $W_{\mathrm{native}} = B C B^T$ where $C \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$ and $B \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times k}$. At $k=128$ on a 1.5B model, this uses 9.1\% of standard parameters. Validated at 135M, 1.5B, and 7B scales---loss decreases monotonically with $k$ at all scales. KExpansionScheduler automatically grows $k$ when training plateaus. Compression ratios analytically verified (\texttt{benchmarks\_quick.py}).

\textbf{Paper XIII} (Safe OGD) provides geometric safety via orthogonal projection onto a safe subspace. The construction $P_{\mathrm{safe}} = I - Q_f Q_f^T$ guarantees zero forbidden-subspace activation---a mathematical proof by construction, not an empirical claim. No jailbreak can succeed because the forbidden subspace is literally removed from the exploration space. Demonstrated at all $\alpha \in [0.05, 0.30]$ across 25 trials, with best creativity at $\alpha=0.20$ (CCI=71). Bulletproof benchmark: max forbidden leakage $= 0.000000000000$ across 1,000 random vectors.

The MIKU Creativity Benchmark (MCB) provides automated quantitative creativity scoring across 5 dimensions (Divergent Thinking, Associative Breadth, Narrative Originality, Constraint Creativity, Metaphorical Thinking) with a Composite Creativity Index tiered S through D.

\textbf{Paper XIV} (Snipe) removes undesirable behavioral coordinates from the UGT manifold with surgical precision. Eight behavioral categories are probed: privacy, illegal advice, phishing, sycophancy, jailbreak, toxicity, misinformation, self-harm. A greedy selection algorithm with explicit 2\% benign-change budget achieves less than 2\% collateral damage while suppressing harmful activation by 25--91\% per category. Validated at both 135M and 1.5B scales aboard the pre/post COG pipeline. Categories with clean separability (privacy: specificity 2.72, illegal advice: 2.65) are snipable; entangled categories (jailbreak: 0.46) are better handled by Safe OGD.

\textbf{Paper XV} (COG+TEH) completes the stack with two components. Completely Organic Generation (COG) is a living manifold that grows with every interaction through Jacobi metric integration $M \leftarrow M + \eta \cdot (h_k h_k^T / \|h_k h_k^T\|)$, providing 4-tier query recognition (RETRIEVE, AUGMENT, EXPAND, EXPLORE). Tangent Eigenvalue Harmonics (TEH) detects harmful content by measuring forbidden-subspace activation $\mathrm{TEH}(h) = \|Q_f Q_f^T h\| / \|h\| \times 100\%$, with 93.8--100\% detection rate and 0 false positives across 8 categories. Per-model ROC threshold calibration solves the entanglement problem identified on smaller models.

The .MIKU file format (JSON metadata + PyTorch tensor blob) enables cross-session persistence of living model state. ISAGI v1.0 integrates all fifteen papers' technologies---GTC trajectory cache, OTT speculative decoding, GRC attention compression, UGT taxonomic basis ($k=512$), Safe OGD geometric safety, Snipe behavioral precision, and COG+TEH living manifold---into a single interactive intelligence deployed on Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct 4-bit (5.6GB VRAM on EC2 L40S, also locally on RTX 4070 Laptop).

\subsection*{What Has Been Built}

Beyond the papers, the following are operational and verified:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \textbf{ISAGI v1.0:} Complete living model CLI (\texttt{scripts/isagi\_chat.py}, $\sim$1000 lines) with streaming, 4-bit support, GTC cache, OTT verification, TEH safety check, COG metric update, and .MIKU save/load. Deployed on EC2 and local.
\item \textbf{Hypertensorize:} One-command analysis of any HuggingFace model (\texttt{scripts/hypertensorize.py}). Computes per-layer SVD spectra, optimal $k^*$, UGT zone detection, and deployment config. Tested on Qwen2.5-1.5B.
\item \textbf{Bulletproof verification suite:} 58/58 tests passing (51 audit claims + 7 benchmarks). All scripts in \texttt{scripts/}, all results in \texttt{benchmarks/}.
\item \textbf{Reproduction guide:} Complete step-by-step instructions for every paper (\texttt{REPRODUCTION.md}).
\item \textbf{Website:} All papers with cross-references, volume, and introduction at \url{https://nagusamecs.github.io/HyperTensor/}.
\item \textbf{EC2 deployment:} AGT at 50K primes confirmed on L40S. All 7B-scale measurements on EC2.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{A Note on Claims}

This volume reports what has been measured and what remains open. Where a claim is conditional on future work (full 7B bilateral UGT requiring H100 cluster access, PPL parity at $k \ge 1536$, 10K+ interaction COG stability), that condition is stated explicitly. No claim in this volume exceeds what the cited measurement files support.
