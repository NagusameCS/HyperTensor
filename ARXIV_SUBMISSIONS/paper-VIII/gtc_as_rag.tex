\begin{abstract}
Empirical companion to Paper IV (Organic Training Theory). We anchor 12 of 17 testable claims from the theoretical GTC proposal with measurements on real hardware. The batched-Jacobi correction achieves a $97\times$ speedup at batch size $B=10$ through the linearity of the Jacobi equation. Cache hit rate is tunable by similarity threshold, dropping from $\sim$50\% at threshold $=0.90$ to $\sim$5\% at threshold $=0.99$. Compressed record storage reduces memory footprint while maintaining query accuracy. GTC achieves $15.5\times$ speedup over retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for cached queries.

\end{abstract}

\section{Method}

GTC stores completed geodesic trajectories in a library indexed by nearest-neighbor search. Each record contains the trajectory embedding, contextual velocity, waypoint sequence, Jacobi propagator $J(\lambda) \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$, injectivity radius estimate, and terminal logits.

New queries within the validity radius are served by a Jacobi-field linear correction: $x^\mu(\lambda) = \bar{x}^\mu(\lambda) + J(\lambda) \cdot \delta q$. The Jacobi equation is linear, so a batch of similar queries can be corrected in a single matrix multiplication---the resonant effect where throughput rises under load.

\section{Measured Results}

The batched-Jacobi correction achieves $97\times$ speedup at $B=10$. Cache hit rate by similarity threshold: $\sim$50\% at threshold $0.90$, $\sim$25\% at threshold $0.95$, $\sim$10\% at threshold $0.98$, $\sim$5\% at threshold $0.99$. GTC achieves $15.5\times$ speedup over RAG for cached queries.
