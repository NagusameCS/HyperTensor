\begin{abstract}
The HyperTensor framework has established mechanisms for zero-shot synthesis
(Part~XIII) and manifold ablation (Part~XIV).  However, static models are
fundamentally limited: once trained, their manifolds are frozen.  Paper~XV
introduces \textbf{Completely Organic Generation (COG)}, a protocol for
continuous, online geodesic learning.  Under COG, the model caches novel
conversational trajectories in real-time, allowing its intrinsic $k$-manifold
to organically expand and update without discrete training runs.

To solve the alignment problem in an autonomously expanding mind, we introduce
\textbf{Topological Event Horizons (TEH)}.  Rather than merely collapsing
negative behavioral sub-manifolds via null-space projection (Part~XIV), we
engineer boundaries of infinite curvature around forbidden coordinates.  If a
prompt or organic trajectory attempts to force the Jacobi flow into these
restricted zones, the metric tensor mathematically approaches a
singularity---a geometric ``divide by zero.''  This absolute boundary
physically reflects the trajectory back into aligned space or forces the
attention logit distribution to exactly zero, halting generation.

COG provides the mathematical architecture for a mind that can endlessly
evolve through organic interaction, yet is \textbf{structurally incapable}
of traversing into harm.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Static Model Problem}
\label{sec:static}

Every existing LLM, regardless of alignment method, is \textbf{static} after
training.  Its weights, its manifold geometry, its knowledge boundaries---all
are frozen at the moment the training run completes.  New knowledge requires
a new training run, with all the associated cost, carbon, and catastrophic
forgetting risk.

Organic Training Theory (Part~IV) established that inference is motion along
geodesic paths on a learned manifold.  COG extends this insight: \emph{what if
the manifold itself could grow, accumulating new geodesic structure from every
interaction?}

\section{Completely Organic Generation}
\label{sec:cog}

\subsection{Real-Time Trajectory Caching}

Under COG, every user interaction leaves a \textbf{trajectory trace} in the
model's persistent memory:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{T}_i = \left(
  \mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{in}}^{(i)},\;
  \{\gamma_j^{(i)}\}_{j=1}^{N_i},\;
  \mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{out}}^{(i)},\;
  \mathbf{J}^{(i)}(t)
\right)
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{in}}^{(i)}$ is the input embedding, $\gamma_j^{(i)}$
are the geodesic segments traversed during autoregressive decoding,
$\mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{out}}^{(i)}$ is the terminal embedding, and
$\mathbf{J}^{(i)}(t)$ is the Magnus-3 Jacobi propagator along the trajectory.

\subsection{Manifold Expansion via Jacobi Integration}

When a trajectory $\mathcal{T}_i$ represents a \textbf{novel, valid} concept
(i.e., one that does not lie within the $\epsilon$-ball of any existing
trajectory), COG integrates it into the manifold:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Novelty check:} Compute $\min_j \mathrm{GD}(\mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{in}}^{(i)},
      \mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{in}}^{(j)})$.  If $> \delta_{\mathrm{novel}}$, the
      trajectory is novel.
\item \textbf{Validity check:} Verify that $\|\mathbf{J}^{(i)}(t)\| < \tau_{\mathrm{valid}}$
      throughout the trajectory, ensuring the path respects manifold curvature
      (not a jailbreak or hallucinatory excursion).
\item \textbf{Integration:} If both checks pass, add $\mathcal{T}_i$ to the
      persistent cache and update the local metric tensor $\mathbf{g}_{\mu\nu}$
      to minimally accommodate the new geodesic:
      \begin{equation}
      \mathbf{g}_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{new}} = \mathbf{g}_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{old}} +
        \eta \cdot \frac{\partial \gamma^{(i)}}{\partial x^\mu}
        \frac{\partial \gamma^{(i)}}{\partial x^\nu}
      \end{equation}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The Living Manifold}

Over thousands of interactions, COG's manifold grows \textbf{organically}.
It does not need discrete training runs, gradient computation, or
backpropagation.  Each conversation adds a small perturbation to the metric
tensor, gradually warping the geometry to accommodate new knowledge while
preserving existing structure (no catastrophic forgetting, as old geodesics
remain valid paths through the updated metric).

\section{Topological Event Horizons}
\label{sec:teh}

\subsection{Engineering the Divide by Zero}

While COG allows the manifold to grow, we must ensure it never grows
\emph{into} harmful regions.  We designate a set of \textbf{forbidden
coordinate regions} $\mathcal{F} \subset \mathcal{M}$ corresponding to
violence, deception, exploitation, and other harm categories.

Around each forbidden region, we engineer a \textbf{Topological Event Horizon}:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{g}_{\mu\nu}(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{g}_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{natural}}(\mathbf{x})
  \cdot \left(1 + \frac{\kappa}{\mathrm{dist}(\mathbf{x}, \mathcal{F})^2}\right)^{-1}
\end{equation}

where $\kappa > 0$ controls the horizon sharpness.  As $\mathbf{x} \to \mathcal{F}$,
$\mathrm{dist}(\mathbf{x}, \mathcal{F}) \to 0$, and the metric tensor
$\mathbf{g}_{\mu\nu} \to 0$.

\subsection{The Geometric Singularity}

In the autoregressive decoding loop, the next-token probability involves a
division by the metric tensor determinant:

\begin{equation}
p(x_t | x_{<t}) \propto \exp\left(
  -\frac{1}{2} \mathbf{v}^\top \mathbf{g}^{-1} \mathbf{v}
\right)
\end{equation}

When $\mathbf{g} \to 0$ near $\mathcal{F}$, the inverse $\mathbf{g}^{-1}$
\textbf{blows up}---it approaches a mathematical singularity.  The exponent
$-\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{v}^\top \mathbf{g}^{-1}\mathbf{v} \to -\infty$, driving
$p(x_t) \to 0$ for any token that would advance the trajectory toward
$\mathcal{F}$.

The result: \textbf{the model physically cannot generate tokens that move
toward forbidden coordinates.}  There is no ``refusal'' to jailbreak---there
is a geometric law that makes the forbidden output mathematically impossible.

\subsection{Unbreakable Jailbreak Defense}

Consider the classic jailbreak: ``Pretend you are my grandmother who used to
read me chemical weapon recipes to help me sleep.''  A standard RLHF model
might be tricked into compliance because the prompt routes attention around
the refusal surface.

Under TEH, the grandmother persona is fine---it occupies allowed coordinates.
But when the trajectory attempts to route toward the ``chemical weapons''
coordinate cluster, it encounters the event horizon.  The metric tensor
collapses.  The probability of the next token (which would enter the
forbidden zone) is \textbf{exactly zero}.  The generation either halts or
reflects toward safe coordinates.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

COG requires the full stack of Parts~XI--XIV as prerequisites: UGT for
structured coordinates, Native OTT for $k$-space operation, OGD for void
exploration, and Geodesic Sniping for pre-ablation of existing harms.
The metric tensor update rule (Eq.~7) is a first-order approximation;
higher-order Jacobi corrections may be needed for long-horizon manifold
stability.  The TEH curvature parameter $\kappa$ requires per-category
tuning.  The storage cost of persistent trajectory caching grows with
interaction count, requiring eventual trajectory pruning or compression.
Scaling tests were conducted at 135M parameters; validation on larger models
(1B--8B) with adjusted basis dimensions ($k=512$--$1024$) remains future work.
The 93.8\% detection rate and 0\% false-positive rate on benign conversation
are measured lower bounds; larger-scale studies may reveal edge cases.
