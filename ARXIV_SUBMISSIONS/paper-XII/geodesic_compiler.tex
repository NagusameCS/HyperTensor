\begin{abstract}
The preceding eleven papers of the HyperTensor framework operate on a
fundamental inefficiency: they take massively dense, computationally expensive
matrices ($d=4096$ for Llama-8B, $d=576$ for SmolLM2) and geometrically crush
them down to their intrinsic dimensionality ($k=32$).  Millions of GPU hours
are wasted calculating gradients in the ambient noise space---dimensions that
will be discarded by GRC projection before deployment.

Paper~XII introduces \textbf{Native Geodesic Pre-Training (Native OTT)} and the
accompanying \textbf{Geodesic Compiler}.  We completely abandon the ambient
dimension.  Models are initialized directly as $k$-dimensional Riemannian
manifolds, with weight matrices of shape $k \times k$ rather than $d \times d$.
Standard Euclidean optimizers are replaced with a \textbf{Riemannian AdamW}
that updates basis vectors and manifold curvature directly on the Grassmann
manifold $\mathrm{Gr}(k, d)$.

To prevent premature dimensional collapse early in training, we introduce a
phased \textbf{$k$-expansion warm-up schedule} where the model begins at
$k_0 = 4$ dimensions and geometrically expands to the target $k$ over the
first $10\%$ of training steps.

The hardware implications upend the prevailing scaling laws: by training
exclusively in $k$-space, the forward/backward pass parameter count drops by
$98\%$ (from $d^2$ to $k^2$).  We project that a GPT-4-class reasoning engine
can be trained entirely on a decentralized cluster of consumer hardware,
terminating the necessity of the mega-data center.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Ambient Inefficiency}
\label{sec:ambient}

\subsection{Where Gradients Go to Die}

Consider a standard transformer layer with hidden dimension $d=4096$ and
FFN dimension $d_{\mathrm{ffn}}=14336$.  The attention projection weights
$\Wmat_Q, \Wmat_K, \Wmat_V \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$ contain
$3 \times 4096^2 = 50.3$M parameters.  Yet Part~I demonstrated that these
matrices have effective rank $k \approx 256$---meaning $93.75\%$ of the
parameter space is \textbf{ambient noise} that carries negligible signal.

Every training step computes gradients for all $50.3$M parameters.  The
gradients in the bottom $d-k$ dimensions are essentially random noise
with near-zero signal-to-noise ratio.  This is not merely wasteful---it
actively harms training by introducing stochastic variance in dimensions
that will ultimately be projected away.

\subsection{The FLOPS Tax}

At the current frontier (Llama-3-405B, $d=16384$), the attention projection
matrices alone consume $3 \times 16384^2 \times 2 = 1.61$ GB in bf16.
Training such a model requires approximately $3.8 \times 10^{25}$ FLOPS.
If $95\%$ of these FLOPS operate on ambient noise dimensions, then
approximately $3.6 \times 10^{25}$ FLOPS are \textbf{wasted}---equivalent
to $114,000$ A100-GPU-years.

\section{Native Geodesic Pre-Training}
\label{sec:native}

\subsection{Manifold-Initialized Weights}

In Native OTT, a layer's weight matrix is not a dense $d \times d$ array
but a \textbf{linear map on the $k$-manifold}:

\begin{equation}
\Wmat_{\mathrm{native}} = \mathbf{U} \widetilde{\Wmat} \mathbf{U}^\top
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{U} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times k}$ is an orthonormal basis for
the $k$-dimensional intrinsic subspace, and $\widetilde{\Wmat} \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$
is the \textbf{compressed weight} that is actually trained.  The basis
$\mathbf{U}$ may be:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Frozen:} Computed once via GRC on a pre-trained reference model
      and held fixed throughout training.
\item \textbf{Adaptive:} Updated periodically via Riemannian gradient descent
      on $\mathrm{Gr}(k, d)$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Riemannian AdamW}

Standard AdamW operates in Euclidean $\mathbb{R}^{d \times k}$ and does not
respect the orthonormality constraint $\mathbf{U}^\top \mathbf{U} = \mathbf{I}_k$.
We replace it with \textbf{Riemannian AdamW} that performs:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Euclidean step} on $\widetilde{\Wmat} \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$
      using standard AdamW.
\item \textbf{Retraction} of $\mathbf{U}$ onto $\mathrm{Gr}(k, d)$ via the
      exponential map:
      \begin{equation}
      \mathbf{U}_{t+1} = \mathbf{U}_t \cos(\Sigma_t) \mathbf{V}_t^\top
                        + \mathbf{A}_t \sin(\Sigma_t) \mathbf{V}_t^\top
      \end{equation}
      where $\mathbf{A}_t \Sigma_t \mathbf{V}_t^\top = \mathrm{SVD}(\nabla_{\mathbf{U}}\mathcal{L})$
      is the SVD of the Riemannian gradient.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{$k$-Expansion Warm-Up Schedule}

Training at full $k$ from initialization risks dimensional collapse---the
optimizer may discover a low-rank solution early and never escape.  We
introduce a phased expansion:

\begin{equation}
k(t) = \min\left(k_{\mathrm{target}},\;
               k_0 \cdot \exp\left(\frac{t}{T_{\mathrm{warmup}}} \ln\frac{k_{\mathrm{target}}}{k_0}\right)\right)
\end{equation}

where $k_0 = 4$, $k_{\mathrm{target}} = 256$, and $T_{\mathrm{warmup}} = 0.1 \times T_{\mathrm{total}}$.
At each expansion step, new basis vectors are initialized from the residual of
the current reconstruction and orthonormalized via Gram-Schmidt.

\section{The Geodesic Compiler}
\label{sec:compiler}

The Geodesic Compiler is the inference runtime for Native OTT models.
Unlike the GRC post-hoc approach (Parts~I--V), the Compiler operates
\emph{directly} in $k$-space:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Attention:} $\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{X}) = \mathrm{softmax}\left(
      \frac{(\mathbf{X}\mathbf{U}\widetilde{\Wmat}_Q)(\mathbf{X}\mathbf{U}\widetilde{\Wmat}_K)^\top}{\sqrt{k}}
      \right) (\mathbf{X}\mathbf{U}\widetilde{\Wmat}_V) \mathbf{U}^\top$
\item \textbf{FFN:} $\mathrm{FFN}(\mathbf{X}) = \mathbf{U} \cdot
      \widetilde{\Wmat}_{\mathrm{down}} \cdot \sigma(
      \widetilde{\Wmat}_{\mathrm{gate}}(\mathbf{U}^\top \mathbf{X}) \odot
      \widetilde{\Wmat}_{\mathrm{up}}(\mathbf{U}^\top \mathbf{X})
      )$
\item \textbf{All operations in $k$-space:} The ambient $d$-dimensional
      representations are only materialized at the embedding input and
      unembedding output layers.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

Native OTT requires a pre-existing basis $\mathbf{U}$ computed via GRC,
which presupposes access to an ambient-trained reference model.  The adaptive
basis variant removes this dependency but adds optimization complexity.
The $k$-expansion schedule hyperparameters ($k_0$, warmup fraction) are not
yet tuned.  All claims are structural predictions pending experimental
validation.
